## Executive summary

The ticketing industry consolidated in 2025. Two of EventLinqs's four locked
competitors lost their independence within six months:

- **DICE** (London, founded 2014) was acquired by Fever in June 2025.
- **Eventbrite** (NYSE: EB, founded 2006) was acquired by Bending Spoons for
  approximately $500M in December 2025, delisting March 2026 with a market
  cap of $452.96M - an 83.65% decline from its 2018 IPO peak of $2.77B.

Only **Live Nation / Ticketmaster** (NYSE: LYV) remains a standalone giant,
with a market cap of ~$38B and 2025 revenue of $25.2B. Live Nation's stock
is up 20.8% over the past 12 months despite ongoing antitrust litigation
and a US court finding that Ticketmaster maintained a harmful monopoly over
major concert venues.

**Humanitix** remains independent in the AU not-for-profit niche with $8.6M
revenue and a model that channels 100% of profits to charity, with no path
to venture-scale growth.

Customer satisfaction is universally low across the incumbents. Ticketmaster
holds a 1.6-star rating across 10,576 customer reviews on PissedConsumer,
with only 13% likely to recommend. Even Eventbrite (third-party NPS rank
amongst its peers) and Live Nation Entertainment outscored Ticketmaster on
NPS rankings, suggesting the entire field is unloved by users.

DICE is the lone exception: its iOS App Store rating is 4.9 stars, with
14,055+ reviewer praise on Trustpilot for "user-friendly interface and the
app's overall design," ticket transfers, waitlists, and transparent pricing.

**The strategic moment:** the consolidation has reduced the number of
independent challengers. The window for a new entrant combining DICE-quality
UX, Eventbrite's marketplace breadth, and Humanitix's community trust is
materially wider in 2026 than it was 18 months ago. The incumbent
(Ticketmaster) is loathed and antitrust-exposed; the consolidators (Fever,
Bending Spoons) are integrating acquisitions; the AU community niche
(Humanitix) is structurally capped.

- Ticketmaster fee-bearing ticket volume: 346 million tickets sold

  already booked

**Stock and market position:**

- Stock price 23 May 2026: ~$166.33

**Customer satisfaction:**

- Ticketmaster average rating across 10,576 reviews (PissedConsumer): 1.6

- 1.4-star average across 458 reviews (SmartCustomer)
- 24.10 / 200 rating on Customer Service Scoreboard
- iOS App Store rating 4.8/5 (the only positive metric, suggests the app
  works but the company doesn't)

**Strategic position:**

- Wins through venue exclusivity and Live Nation parent's concert promotion

**Strategic position:**

- Mobile-app-first ticketing
- Premium music/club/comedy focus, not stadium/sport
- Transparent pricing ("The price you'll pay. No surprises later.")
- Phone-number-based account (no usernames or passwords)
- Hand-drawn brand illustrations, editorial typography (Foggy display

- Partnership with cultural venues (Alexandra Palace, Newport Jazz,
  Sonar, Rough Trade globally)

**User feedback themes (Trustpilot, App Store):**

- "Clean, logical interface" / "user-friendly" - dominant theme
- Ticket transfers and waitlists described as "easy" and "convenient"
- Support team praised for fast email response on event-day issues

- $400M valuation cap despite great product - design alone couldn't

- The acquisition explicitly cited DICE as enabling Fever to be "the

**The DICE lesson for EventLinqs:**

- Beautiful design is necessary but not sufficient
- Marketplace breadth is the scaling layer
- The combination of design + breadth is the gap in the market - which
  is the EventLinqs opportunity

## Industry-level patterns

### Pattern 1: Consolidation

In 12 months, two of four locked competitors lost independence. The
acquirers (Fever for DICE, Bending Spoons for Eventbrite) are integrating.
This means:

- Less competitive innovation in 2026 than in 2024
- Window for a fresh entrant with focused execution
- Future acquirers will be looking for the next DICE or Eventbrite to buy
- EventLinqs has a path: build, scale, either remain independent (Live
  Nation path) or be acquired (DICE / Eventbrite path), or anchor in a
  niche (Humanitix path).

### Pattern 2: Customer hatred is universal except for DICE

Across all major ticketing platforms, customer satisfaction is poor:

- Ticketmaster: 1.6 stars, 13% recommendation rate
- Eventbrite: middling NPS, declining revenue
- DICE: 4.9 App Store stars, only standout

The user-experience moat is real. EventLinqs that ships DICE-quality UX
on a Ticketmaster-scale marketplace would be commercially unprecedented.

### Pattern 3: Margins concentrate in ticketing fees, not in event promotion

Live Nation's concert business runs at 3.3% margin. Ticketmaster's
ticketing business runs at 37%. Sponsorship runs at 64%. The platform
business model where EventLinqs sits (ticketing fees + future sponsorship)
is structurally more profitable than concert promotion.

EventLinqs's 2% + $0.50 / ticket pricing puts it in a sustainable margin
zone if scale is achieved.

### Pattern 4: Antitrust pressure on the incumbent

Live Nation/Ticketmaster faces an active US Department of Justice antitrust
case. Internal documents surfaced in the trial showed employee contempt for
customers. If the DOJ wins meaningful structural remedies (forced
divestiture of Ticketmaster), the entire competitive landscape opens up.

EventLinqs should position with this in mind - "we are the alternative" is
a positioning that resonates with the moment.

## Implications for EventLinqs

### What to take from each competitor

| Source | What to take | What to reject |
|---|---|---|
| Ticketmaster | Marketplace breadth, venue partnership ambition, transaction reliability, deferred revenue model | Hidden fees, opaque pricing, customer contempt, monopoly mindset |
| DICE | Editorial design quality, transparent pricing, phone-number auth, hand-drawn brand voice, mobile-first | Niche-only positioning, app-only distribution, premium-music narrowness |
| Eventbrite | Self-serve creator marketplace, 88M MAU breadth, ads platform optionality, broad category coverage | Product stagnation, design generic-ness, fee elimination as growth strategy |
| Humanitix | Community trust, cultural focus, transparent purpose, AU-first positioning | NFP ceiling, capped scale, limited venture optionality |

### Positioning statement (data-grounded)

EventLinqs is a commercial marketplace combining DICE-quality user
experience with Eventbrite-scale breadth and Humanitix-grade community
trust, positioned to capture the post-consolidation window as Ticketmaster's
antitrust liability creates structural disruption.

We sell the same thing the incumbents sell - tickets - but with three real
differentiators:

1. **Transparent pricing on every page** (matches DICE, surpasses
   Ticketmaster). EventLinqs's 2% + $0.50 / ticket is stated upfront, with
   organiser take-home shown.
2. **Community-first content layers** that none of the competitors offer:
   cultural calendar, sensitivity markers, partnership badges, First
   Nations positioning. These layer on top of marketplace breadth.
3. **DICE-quality execution on a marketplace surface area** - typography,
   spacing, motion, photography, microcopy at premium-product standard
   across every event card, every browse page, every checkout step.

### The acquisition reality

Both DICE and Eventbrite were eventually acquired. EventLinqs should plan
for the same possibility - which means building for an acquirer's
diligence: clean financials, sustainable unit economics, defensible IP,
clear strategic moat (community-first content layers are the moat).

Or build for independence on the Live Nation path - which requires venue
exclusivity deals at scale. Premature to commit to either path; build for
optionality.
