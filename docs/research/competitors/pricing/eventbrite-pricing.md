ANALYST NOTE: Eventbrite is the clearest public-pricing model in the set and the most directly comparable to EventLinqs. Key patterns: (1) Free-events-free is the headline promise, repeated three times (hero, mid-page heading, FAQ). (2) Pricing is delivered as three plain cards, not a dense table - free / service fee / processing fee. (3) Fees are buyer-paid by default with an organiser opt-in to absorb - this is the de-facto industry norm. (4) Discovery/marketplace reach (90M) is the primary value lever, sold harder than low fees. (5) An explicit head-to-head competitor table is used as a trust/positioning weapon. (6) Scale proof (270M tickets, 89M users, 4.7M events) sits directly above the pricing to pre-justify the fee. Weakness: the "3.7% + $1.79" plus "2.9%" split forces the reader to do mental math - there is no single all-in number and no worked example, so the true cost is partially obscured despite the "simple" claim. AU-specific: AUD shown, AU listed in supported markets, AUD $15/mo Pro plan.
