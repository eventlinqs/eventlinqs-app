ANALYST NOTE: Squarespace is the odd-one-out and was clearly chosen for structural contrast, not direct rivalry. It is a website builder, not a ticketing platform - selling tickets means manually adding an event as a "service product", and Squarespace Payments is not even available in Australia. The instructive contrast: Squarespace monetises via fixed monthly subscription ($19-$109/mo) plus card processing, with the per-transaction fee falling as you pay more upfront - the inverse of the ticketing industry's "free to start, fee per ticket sold" model. Pricing page craft is strong though: clean 4-card layout, annual/monthly toggle with explicit "Save X% annually", a "Recommended" badge on Core to anchor choice, expandable full feature matrix for the detail-hungry without cluttering the default view, and full-answer FAQs (not just titles). Lessons worth stealing: the recommended-tier anchor, the savings-framed billing toggle, and the progressive-disclosure feature matrix. Lesson to avoid: subscription-first pricing is hostile to the long tail of community/one-off organisers EventLinqs must win - it taxes people before they have sold a single ticket.
