# Outreach templates - aligned to the certified platform (2026-07-11)

The working scripts for growth lever 1: recruit the first 25 to 50 organisers
personally and concierge-onboard each. Lead hooks are THE EVENT LAUNCH KIT and
MAGIC START. Switch triggers: SEATING for seated-venue organisers,
PROOF-OF-DRAW for performers. Geelong and Melbourne are ONE launch territory
(an hour apart, one crowd); every other city collects demand through the
waitlist.

## Claims discipline (binding on every message)

- May claim, verified on staging: build your event, map your room, get your
  complete promo kit in minutes, free; describe or speak your event and the
  draft builds itself; publish in under a minute; any room shape including
  tables and whole-table booking in one tap; buyers can move their own seats;
  organiser-assigns seating; attendees are emailed on seat changes; performers
  see exactly how many tickets their sharing sold; you own every attendee
  relationship and can export it; all-in price shown before checkout; free
  events are free.
- Never name a competitor in anything sent or posted.
- Never claim: recurring events, phone wallet passes, built-in email
  campaigns, offline door scanning, or being the cheapest all-in.
- Do NOT market the gig board or the performer directory yet. They are built
  and switched off; they are the planned post-launch "performers, bring your
  numbers" moment.
- Copy laws apply to every send: Australian English, no exclamation marks, no
  em or en dashes, community never the banned c-word.

## Template 1 - seated-venue organiser (comedy rooms, theatres, dinner shows)

Trigger: seating. Channel: email or DM. Personalise the first line to their
room and a recent show.

Subject: Your seat map, built once, working for every show at [venue]

Hi [name],

I watched [recent show] sell at [venue] and noticed buyers could not pick
their exact seat. I have built an Australian ticketing platform where they
can, and where the room works the way you already run it.

You draw [venue]'s layout once: rows, tables, standing areas, accessible
seats, your own numbering. Then every event reuses it. Buyers pick their seat
on the map, or you can sell first and assign seats yourself afterwards. If
plans change, you can move any attendee and they are emailed their new seat
automatically, or you can let people move themselves to any open seat.
Whole-table bookings happen in one tap for gala nights.

Publishing takes minutes: describe the show in a sentence, the draft builds
itself, and the moment you publish you get your complete promo kit, which is
your live page, a print-ready QR poster for the door, share cards, and
tracked links that show you exactly which channel sells.

Your attendee list is yours: export it any time, no walls.

I am onboarding the first [Geelong/Melbourne] venues personally, free, and I
will build your seat map with you on a call. Fifteen minutes this week?

[sign-off]
