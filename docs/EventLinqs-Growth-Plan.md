# EventLinqs Launch Growth Plan (locked 2026, carry to all chats)

Grounded in the proven playbooks of Eventbrite, Humanitix, DICE, and two-sided
marketplace cold-start research. This is how EventLinqs wins. The repo is the source of
truth, not memory. Dash rule followed in prose to Lawal.

---

## THE PROVEN LAW (do not violate)

Neither Eventbrite nor Humanitix won on ads or influencers. They won on three things:
a built-in viral loop, a sharp wedge that made switching obvious, and relentless
supply-side (organiser) recruitment. Paid marketing was minor and late. Two-thirds of
failed marketplaces die on the supply side because founders chase demand volume instead
of liquidity. The fix: build the harder side (organisers) first, chase density not reach,
do things that do not scale, and earn the right to advertise only after density.

For a solo bootstrapped founder, paid ads and influencer sponsorships are the WRONG
primary lever and actively waste money before density exists.

---

## THE WEDGE (the one-sentence switching reason)

"EventLinqs puts your event in front of the right attendees through our discovery feed
and push alerts, AND you own every attendee relationship: no walled gardens, no withheld
emails."

Two edges, both grounded in what organisers complain about in 2026:
- DISCOVERY UPSIDE: DICE gets ~40% of sales from discovery + push. EventLinqs offers the
  same demand engine to organisers who list, which Eventbrite's marketplace does not push
  the same way and which a shrinking Eventbrite is less able to deliver post-acquisition.
- DATA OWNERSHIP: DICE does not share attendee emails; Eventbrite and others limit it.
  This kills an organiser's ability to build their own audience. EventLinqs gives the
  organiser full attendee data ownership: they own their customers, we never wall them off.
  This is a concrete, provable switching reason that costs nothing and the incumbents
  structurally cannot easily match.

TIMING EDGE (use it now): Eventbrite was acquired by Bending Spoons (March 2026, ~$500M),
taken private, cutting staff, with reports the free tier may be removed. Organisers are
actively evaluating alternatives. Launch into that opening.

DO NOT copy Humanitix's funding model: they are a charity, so they got Google Ad Grants,
$1M Google Impact Challenge, Atlassian and NSW Government grants. As a sole trader you get
none of that. Copy their wedge-story discipline and organiser pricing, not their funding.

## THE TWO ENGINES (they are DIFFERENT, build both)

### 1. The ACQUISITION LOOP (growth: brings new users in, near-zero cost)
This is what Eventbrite actually grew on. Two sub-loops:
- SHARE-A-TICKET: after buying, the attendee is prompted to share the event with a
  personalised invite link that tracks who joined through it. Every share markets the
  event AND EventLinqs to non-users. Make this as frictionless as the discovery feed.
- INVITE-AN-ORGANISER (attendee to organiser conversion): after purchase, and in the
  attendee's account, a prompt: "Run your own events? It is free to start." This was
  Eventbrite's single biggest growth hack: buyers discovering they can become organisers.
- Track invite attribution so we can see which shares and which organisers drive signups.

### 2. The DEMAND ENGINE (retention + discovery: keeps users coming back)
- Attendee taste/follow graph (built natively, no Spotify).
- Personalised discovery feed.
- Alert engine: PWA web push (primary, ~5x email conversion per DICE) plus email backbone.
- Who's-going social proof and follow-organisers (scope v5 core).
The AI tuning layer (lookalike, predictive demand, campaign autopilot, fill-gap) sharpens
as real data arrives, but the engine is live day one because it IS the organiser pitch.

NOTE: the demand engine is discovery + retention; the acquisition loop is growth. The
research is explicit that Eventbrite's virality came from the invite/share mechanism, NOT
the discovery marketplace. Both are required; do not conflate them.

## SEO (the quiet compounding engine)

Eventbrite's event pages ranked in Google for years of free organic traffic. EventLinqs
already has bespoke community-by-city intersection pages: this is the same play. Ensure
Schema.org Event structured data, clean URLs, fast pages, sitemap submitted to Google
Search Console day one. One paid month of Semrush or Ahrefs later when attacking
programmatic SEO across scene-city pages, not before.

Launch the platform nationally (anyone in Australia can use it), but concentrate
RECRUITMENT and SEEDING effort on one wedge where Lawal has real reach: Geelong and
Melbourne music and community scenes (DJ ties, local relationships). Depth before breadth
is the single most repeated lesson in the research. National launch decision kept; effort
pointed where it can actually hit density and flip the network.

## SUPPLY-SIDE RECRUITMENT (the highest-leverage work, do by hand)

- Recruit the first 25 to 50 organisers personally (cold outreach, existing network, the
  communities Scenes V2 already maps). Concierge-onboard each one.
- Each organiser brings their own audience as your demand, for free: cheapest demand ever.
- The "influencers" worth your time are NOT Instagram personalities: they are local event
  organisers, promoters, community leaders, and DJs who already own an audience. Signing
  one is supply AND demand in a single move. Lawal's DJ background is a real asset here.
- SEEDING SUBSIDY: offer early organisers a fee holiday or reduced take-rate, then raise it
  once you have liquidity. Straight from the marketplace playbook.
- Founders who write publicly about their supply-side problem recruit supply 3 to 5x
  faster (relevant to the separate YouTube track, kept in its own chat).
